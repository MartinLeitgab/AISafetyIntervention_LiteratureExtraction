Summary  
Data source overview: The paper reports an empirical comparison of three uncertainty–handling algorithms (simple Bayes, odds-likelihood, naïve Dempster-Shafer) inside Pathfinder, a medical expert system that diagnoses lymph-node disease.  Using a decision-theoretic utility model (micromorts) and an expert-rating scale, the authors show that simple Bayes combined with a Maximum-Expected-Utility (MEU) decision rule outperforms the other approaches; naïve D-S is markedly inferior.  They also describe design tactics such as clustering correlated features, and introduce a decision-theoretic evaluation framework.
