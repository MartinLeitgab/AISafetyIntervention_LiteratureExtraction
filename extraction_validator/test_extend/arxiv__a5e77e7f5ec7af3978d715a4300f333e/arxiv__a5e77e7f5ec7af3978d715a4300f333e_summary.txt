Summary  
Data source overview: The paper proposes two distributed‐learning algorithms that enable a network of heterogeneous agents (with different feature dimensions) to cooperate on a binary-classification task.  One algorithm enforces global graph-wide smoothness of predicted labels through a Laplacian regulariser, while the other enforces local neighbourhood smoothness by averaging neighbours’ predictions that share the same true label.  Extensive simulations and a real-world weather dataset show that both cooperative strategies reduce test error compared with non-cooperative training.
