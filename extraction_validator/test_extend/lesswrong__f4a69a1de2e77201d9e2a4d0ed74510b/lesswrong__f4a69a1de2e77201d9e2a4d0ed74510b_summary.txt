Summary  
Data source overview: The blog-post presents a toy Monte-Carlo life-trajectory model that combines (1) actuarial mortality tables, (2) a Metaculus-based distribution over AGI arrival, (3) a time-dependent probability of global catastrophe from misaligned AGI, and (4) chances of curing biological aging once AGI exists.  The author finds the dominant outcomes for a person born in 2001 are “die from AGI” (35 %) or “survive AGI and see aging cured” (43 %).  They discuss which personal and societal actions move probability mass between outcomes, concluding that (i) working on AI safety, (ii) adopting healthy-living habits, and (iii) possibly slowing AGI are the most plausible interventions.
